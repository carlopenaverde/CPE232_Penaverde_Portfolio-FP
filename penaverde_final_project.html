Reflection and Learnings

In managing enterprise servers, the most important kind of learning is technical learning. This includes knowledge of server hardware, operating systems, server configuration and administration, network protocols, storage systems, virtualization, security, and monitoring systems. Additionally, it is important to stay up to date on new technologies and industry best practices. Soft skills such as communication, problem-solving, and collaboration are also important for successful server management.


My Experience

I recently had the opportunity to attend an onsite class at my Course and that is CPE 232 - Managing Enterprise Servers. It was an incredibly rewarding experience. From the moment I stepped into the classroom, I felt a sense of anticipation and excitement. The professor was extremely knowledgeable and made the class engaging and informative. The class was interactive and encouraged us to think critically and ask questions. We discussed a variety of topics, from current affairs to technology. The professor provided us with many resources to help us understand the material, and even gave us the chance to practice what we had learned. At the end of the class, I felt inspired and was looking forward to applying what I had learned in my everyday life. It was a great experience, and I am grateful to have had the opportunity to attend an onsite class.
